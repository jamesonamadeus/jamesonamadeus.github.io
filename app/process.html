i

mmerse

Before we can properly build a solution, we must find the problem. We immerse ourselves in a setting that provides us with the root information that we will need going forward, a user’s experience. In order to understand and empathize with a user, who is facing these pain points, we will need to ask and, most importantly, listen to his/her journey. From positioning ourselves in the perspective of the user, we will gain knowledge of the user’s relationship to the problem and motivation for a possible solution. Only then can we move onto the next step to explore the possibilities of a proper resolution.

e

xplore

We have gained the vital information that we will use as our compass as we explore and diverge on the possibilities of a solution. From researching the competitors, who are already in the industry space and providing a solution to the user’s pain points, we will analyze on why and what makes their value proposition so unique. Once we have learned the competitors' propositions, we will dig deep to find their shortcomings and how they can be improved. From the discovery of the competitors’ weaknesses, we will then use it as our strengths in formulating potential solutions that will meet the needs of our user. Once we have converged the solution prospects to a focal point, we will iterate a functional prototype to test our assumptions.

i

terate

During this phase, we will be testing our Most Dangerous Assumptions (MDAs), which will enable us to create our initial Minimal Viable Product (MVP). Generally, the top 5 presumptions will be considered as the MDAs. We want to be efficient during this process, but not cut corners that can compromise our results. The iterations will be minimal in terms of functionality, like an on and off switch, it should perform the core action and nothing more. Once the iterations begin to demonstrate results that are in correlation with the user’s needs, then we are on the path of creating our first fully functional MVP. It is important and to note that like the iterations, the MVP will be fundamental. If you are not embarrassed by your first release for testing, then you have spent too much valuable time on it. When our MVP is fully functional in terms of meeting the user’s goal(s), we move to our last step of our process - refining the MVP for production.

r

efine

At this point, we have iterated and built a barebone, fully functional MVP that solves our user’s problem(s). Going forward, we will implement UX/UI strategies that will offer the user a more enjoyable MVP experience. From incorporating thoughtful UI design and engineering, we can strive to maximize the usability and experience for the user. The refine objective is to simplify the user’s interaction and create a more efficient process, in terms of accomplishing the user goal(s). Once we maintain a proper balance of visual design and technical functionality for the MVP, where it is operational and adaptable, we will be able to move it into production for public release.
